Background of the Problem


Children are among the most vulnerable members of society.
Unfortunately, they are often easy targets of abuse, exploitation, and neglect, sometimes even within their own families, where they should feel safest.

Abuse comes in many forms:
physical violence, sexual exploitation, child labor, neglect, and emotional mistreatment.
According to global studies, a child loses their life to violence every four minutes, showing the tragic scale of the problem.
Violence against children knows no boundaries.
It can occur anytime and anywhere:
At home, in schools, on the streets, in institutions, and even online.

Every child has the right to grow up in a safe and nurturing environment
where they can fully develop into strong, healthy, and capable adults.
However, this is not the reality for millions of children around the world.
Children born in low and middle-income countries face far greater risks
due to poverty, limited access to healthcare, lack of education,
and weak protective systems.

The gap in survival and well-being is staggering, and without proper
awareness and action, these inequalities will only continue.
Protecting children from harm is not just a responsibility,
it is a moral obligation for all of society.



What is being done about it in the Philippines?


In the Philippines, child safety and health remain pressing issues.
A shocking reality is that a childâ€™s birth is considered one of the
most dangerous moments of their life due to the high risks linked to
poor healthcare access and malnutrition.
Many Filipino children do not survive their first year, and those who do often face lifelong challenges caused by poor nutrition and unsafe environments.

Statistics reveal that the Philippines ranks ninth worldwide in terms of the number of stunted children. Those kids who are too small for their age due to malnutrition.
Around one in three Filipino children suffers from stunting, a condition that not only affects their physical growth but also their cognitive development, making it harder for them to succeed in school and later in life.
While the government and NGOs have launched feeding programs, health campaigns, and protective laws, the challenges remain immense.
Addressing this issue requires consistent community involvement, better health services, and stronger child protection systems that truly reach the most vulnerable families.

Purpose of My Page


The main purpose of my page is to raise awareness and spark meaningful conversations
about child safety, health, and protection. It aims to shed light on the dangers and
challenges children face, particularly in urban areas where poverty, abuse,
and neglect are often hidden behind closed doors. By providing information, statistics,
and solutions, the page hopes to serve as a platform where people can learn about the
real issues affecting children and how they can help.

This advocacy page will also highlight existing support systems and solutions.
Such as hotlines, community programs, and organizations that individuals and
families can access. While child abuse and neglect remain widespread and complex
problems, awareness is the first step toward change. By spreading knowledge,
encouraging dialogue, and offering resources, this page intends to inspire action.
Children deserve to feel safe, to be loved, and to live without fear.
Together, we can protect their innocence and help them grow up in a world where
they are truly valued and cared for.
